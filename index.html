FAQ

Common Questions

What adapter do I need?

BimmerFix works with three adapter types: MHD WiFi (recommended for most users — connects via WiFi hotspot), ENET cable (for F/G/I chassis — connects via Ethernet-to-OBD), and K+DCAN (for E-chassis — connects via Bluetooth serial). You can buy any of these from Amazon or specialized BMW parts suppliers for $20–$80.

Which BMWs are supported?

All BMW models from 2005+ with an OBD-II port. This includes E-chassis (E60, E82, E87, E90, E92, E93), F-chassis (F10, F20, F30, F80, F82, F87), G-chassis (G20, G30, G80, G82, G87), and I-chassis electric models. Both gasoline and diesel engines are supported.

Can I clear fault codes?

Yes. BimmerFix can clear DTCs from individual ECUs or all modules at once. It opens an extended diagnostic session, sends the UDS ClearDiagnosticInformation command (service 0x14), and confirms the clear. Note: clearing codes does not fix the underlying issue — always diagnose and repair first.

What live data can I see?

For the DME (engine computer): RPM, coolant temp, oil temp, intake temp, boost pressure, throttle position, engine load, mass airflow, battery voltage, fuel pressure, timing advance, lambda/AFR, knock counts, and camshaft position. Data rates up to 20 Hz depending on the number of parameters.

Does it support coding?

Yes. BimmerFix supports BMW module coding — change comfort settings, enable features, and customize your vehicle's behavior through the coding module available on Pro and Shop plans. Flash tuning is on the roadmap for a future release.
